# 🧭 HR Analytics Dashboard — Employee Attrition Analysis  

## 📊 Overview  
This project focuses on analyzing **employee attrition (turnover)** using **Power BI**. It helps HR professionals gain insights into the factors influencing attrition, such as **age, gender, education, salary, job role, and years of service**.  
The dashboard enables **data-driven decision-making** for employee retention and engagement strategies.  

---

## 🎯 Objectives  
- Analyze employee attrition trends within the organization.  
- Identify key factors influencing attrition (age, salary, education, etc.).  
- Support HR teams in designing retention strategies.  
- Provide an **interactive and visual dashboard** for decision-making.  

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies  
| Tool / Technology | Purpose |
|-------------------|----------|
| **Microsoft Power BI** | Dashboard creation & visualization |
| **Excel / CSV** | Data source |
| **Power Query Editor** | Data cleaning & transformation |
| **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)** | Measures and calculations (e.g., attrition rate) |

---

## 📂 Dataset Description  
The dataset contains **1,470 employee records** with attributes such as:  
- Employee ID  
- Age  
- Gender  
- Department  
- Education Field  
- Salary Slab  
- Years at Company  
- Job Role  
- Attrition (Yes/No)  

---

## 📈 Dashboard Features  

### **Key Metrics (KPIs):**
- Total Employees: 1470  
- Employees Left: 237  
- Average Age: 37 years  
- Average Salary: 6.5K  
- Average Years at Company: 7 years  
- Attrition Rate  

### **Attrition Analysis by Dimensions:**
- **Education:** Life Sciences (41%) and Medical (32%)  
- **Age Group:** 26–35 years have highest attrition  
- **Salary Slab:** Up to 5K shows maximum attrition  
- **Years at Company:** Peak attrition at 5 years  
- **Gender:** Male (767) | Female (489)  
- **Job Role:** Sales Executive & Research Scientist face highest attrition  

---

## 🔍 Insights & Findings  
- **Mid-career employees (26–35 years)** show the highest attrition.  
- **Low salary brackets** correlate with higher turnover.  
- **Sales & Research roles** face the most pressure and exits.  
- Attrition peaks at **5 years of service**, signaling retention issues.  

---

## 🧩 Conclusion  
The Power BI dashboard successfully highlights critical attrition patterns, enabling HR departments to:  
- Improve compensation policies.  
- Support early-career employees.  
- Focus on high-risk roles.  
- Design retention programs for employees around the 5-year mark.
